Our Objective

The scope of service envisaged is wide-ranging. The activities of "Manav Sewa Dal" focus primarily on the following general objectives:

Disseminating the practical knowledge of the soul.

What is the Power which energizes you? What is the mystery behind the process of respiration? What is the vital energy on which everything depends? That life-energy isn’t something which can be expressed verbally, yet it is the source of all languages. It is invisible, yet it makes us see. It cannot be known through logic or philosophy, yet the powers of reason and memory spring from it. It is the Self in every single being and experiencing it, realizing it, is our natural Dharam, or Manav Dharam (‘the Dharam of mankind’).

Service to all without any discrimination.

Manav Seva Dal is an organization of selfless, selfless, dedicated and disciplined volunteers, who rise above differences of caste, sectarian, religious and spiritual fanaticism, considers human service as their worship, worship and yajan. He is sensitive not only to humans, but also to animals. They are always ready, dynamic and determined to do every kind of service as much as possible. Such is the one who has an innate nature, pride, satisfaction and is imbued with social, secular, national feelings and spiritual qualities, only an organization of volunteers full of such sensitive and analytical personality is truly selfless in the service of human beings and beings. may be moving forward.

Awakening the unlimited potential within each individual

Dharma is the practical experience of God. When a dumb man tastes candy, he experiences its sweetness, but he is incapable of describing it. Similarly, someone who has the divine experience forgets himself totally and is silent – this state of silence is ‘dharma’. So, dharma is the experience of the Self which is within each one of us. Swami Vivekananda said, “Religion means realization”. Dharma is embodying this experience in one’s life. In other words, dharma is not book-knowledge but the practical experience of the Self and this experience is called Atma-Gyan or self-realization. It is the heart and soul of dharma. It is the basis of all religions; it is religion in its purest form. By bringing it into our lives we are not only evolving ourselves but helping the human race to evolve. Nowadays, we have restricted ourselves, we have encased ourselves in the walls of religions and call ourselves Muslims, Christians, Sikhs or Buddhists, but before being Hindu, Muslim, Sikh, Christian, etc. we are human beings, so what is what is the “human religion”?

About Us

One of the great humanitarians of the present time, Shri Satpal Maharaj, is famous all over the world for his inspiring example in the spiritual and social fields as he spreads the message of "Manav Dharam". He lives to serve others, irrespective of caste, colour, creed or religion, in any capacity that he can. In 1976 Shri Satpal Maharaj formed “Manav Sewa Dal” as a service wing of “Manav Utthan Sewa Samiti” .

In 1985 “Manav Sewa Dal” became a registered and recognised social welfare organisation.

Social Initiatives

Manav Sewa Dal", by its very nature, has an unlimited range of social activities. So far it has successfully engaged in the following:

Co-ordinating programs

Co-ordinating programs promoting Manav Dharma on a national basis. Identifying and campaigning against social evils such as alcoholism and the dowry system.

Mission Education

We support the education of underprivileged students across the world. It was started by Shradhey Ji and Suyesh Ji, the sons of Spiritual Master Shri Satpal Ji Maharaj in 2014.

Medical Camp

Mobilising disaster-relief teams to provide free medical treatment as well as emergency clothing, blankets and food aid to disaster victims.

Foodgrain Distribution

Mobilising disaster-relief teams to provide free medical treatment as well as emergency clothing, blankets and food aid to disaster victims. Cleanliness campaigns in villages, towns and hospitals.

Organizing training camps and workshops

Organizing training camps and workshops for its members to help them serve the public more efficiently.

Service with Police

Co-ordinating programs promoting Manav Dharma on a national basis. Identifying and campaigning against social evils such as alcoholism and the dowry system.

Society has always been benefited and uplifted by generous and compassionate individuals who give freely of their time, skills and resources to help those in need. Such services are made possible and practical only by the availability and willingness of volunteers who are motivated simply by natural human kindness and empathy with others less fortunate than themselves. The potential of this positive side of human nature is readily apparent when disaster strikes and people set aside their normal prejudices and attitudes and rally to help the victims. Such a sympathetic reaction is spontaneous and indicates that the potential for service without any selfish motives is inherent in all human beings.
